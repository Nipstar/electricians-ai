Sound Familiar?

These problems cost electricians money every day

From sole trader sparkies to multi-van electrical contractors — if you're doing the work yourself, you know exactly how these problems drain your revenue and steal your time.

Missed Calls = Missed Jobs

You're mid-first-fix on a rewire and the phone buzzes. You can't answer — you're working in a live panel. By the time you call back at lunch, they've already booked another electrician. One in three trades businesses miss incoming calls, and 69% of those callers never leave a voicemail. Every unanswered ring is a £200-£2,000 job walking away.

Evenings and Weekends Lost to Admin

After a full day of installations and callouts, the last thing you want is to spend your evening returning missed calls, typing up quotes, and chasing leads. But if you don't, tomorrow's diary stays empty. You didn't become an electrician to sit at a desk — yet the admin never stops.

Can't Justify Hiring a Receptionist

A full-time receptionist costs £22,000-£28,000 a year. For a sole trader or small electrical firm, that's a huge overhead for someone who only needs to be on the phone a few hours a day. But the alternative — missing calls and losing work — costs you even more.
